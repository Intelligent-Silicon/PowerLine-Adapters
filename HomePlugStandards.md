# HomePlug Standards

### [HomePlug AV2 (HomePlug AV2-MIMO (Multiple-Input Multiple-Output))](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HomePlug#HomePlug_AV2)
 
Introduced in 2012, the current, high-speed standard designed for Gigabit performance, utilizing multiple connections for better stability and speed.

### [HomePlug AV](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HomePlug#HomePlug_AV)

Introduced in 2005, a slower standard that supports speeds up to 600 Mbps, adequate for HD streaming but superseded by AV2 for higher bandwidth needs.

### [HomePlug 1.0](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HomePlug#HomePlug_1.0)

Introduced in 2001, HomePlug 1.0 is the foundational powerline networking standard that enables data transmission at speeds up to 14 Mbps (or 85 Mbps for Turbo versions) by using existing residential electrical wiring to create an Ethernet-based home network. Not suitable for HD Streaming 

### [IEEE 1901](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IEEE_1901)

A broad, international standard for broadband over power lines, ensuring compatibility between different manufacturers' devices, even if they use different HomePlug variations.

### [G.hn (Gigabit Home Networking)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G.hn)

A newer, ITU-T standard that competes with HomePlug. A rival specification for wired home networking that supports speeds up to 2 Gbit/s and operates over four types of legacy wires: telephone wiring, coaxial cables, power lines and plastic optical fiber.
